Energy Drinks vs. Sleep Quality

Hypothesis: As energy drink consumption (within 6 hours before bed) increases, sleep quality decreases. For adequate sleep duration (8+ hours), this negative effect becomes more apparent.

This is one of my best scatterplots because it directly tests whether energy drinks impact sleep quality independently of sleep duration. By using Array.filter() to isolate nights with adequate sleep (8+ hours), we eliminate sleep deprivation as a confounding variable and can clearly see energy drinks' effect on quality. The Array.sort() function organizes data by sleep hours, creating a visual gradient that reveals an interesting pattern: even with long sleep, high energy drink consumption consistently produces smaller circles (lower quality). The color gradient for afternoon energy adds another dimension, showing that poor sleep quality from energy drinks also correlates with next-day fatigue.
